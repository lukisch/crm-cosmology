Titel:
Game-Theoretic Cosmology and the Curvature Feedback Model: Nash Equilibria as Explanatory Framework for Accelerated Expansion
(Spieltheoretische Kosmologie und das Krümmungs-Rückgabepotential-Modell)

Autoren:
Geiger, Lukas

Abstract:
This paper introduces the Curvature Feedback Model (CFM), a game-theoretic framework for cosmology where the universe's expansion is modeled as a Nash equilibrium between a metastable quantum vacuum ("null space") and a spacetime bubble. Unlike LambdaCDM, the CFM explains accelerated expansion not through dark energy, but via a diminishing curvature return potential—a geometric "memory" of the Big Bang. Numerical tests against simulated Type Ia supernova data favor CFM over LambdaCDM ($\Delta\chi^2 \approx -4.8$). The model predicts a time-varying equation of state $w(z)$ testable by the Euclid and Roman space telescopes.
