UPLOAD 1: Paper I (Grundlegung)

Titel:
Game-Theoretic Cosmology and the Curvature Feedback Model:
Nash Equilibria Between Null Space and Spacetime Bubble

Autoren:
Geiger, Lukas (Independent Researcher, Bernau im Schwarzwald, Germany)

Abstract:
We introduce the Curvature Feedback Model (CFM), a game-theoretic
framework in which the universe's accelerated expansion emerges as a
Nash equilibrium between a metastable quantum vacuum (null space) and
a spacetime bubble. The null space seeks to maintain its integrity,
while the spacetime bubble seeks to neutralize the energy gradient
through expansion. The resulting equilibrium produces a time-dependent
curvature return potential Omega_Phi(a) governed by a saturation ODE,
whose solution is a tanh-type function replacing the cosmological
constant Lambda. Tested against 1,590 Pantheon+ Type Ia supernovae
with full covariance matrix, the CFM yields chi^2 = 716.8
(Delta chi^2 = -12.2 vs. LambdaCDM) with a later onset of cosmic
acceleration (z_acc = 0.52 vs. 0.84). The model predicts earlier and
more efficient structure formation, consistent with JWST high-redshift
galaxy observations, the El Gordo cluster anomaly, and early
protoclusters. We provide the game-theoretic derivation, numerical
analysis, and a roadmap for CMB and BAO validation.

Keywords:
cosmology; dark energy; Nash equilibrium; game theory;
Curvature Feedback Model; Friedmann equation; Pantheon+;
Type Ia supernovae; modified gravity; structure formation

UPLOAD 2: Paper II (Flaggschiff)
===============================================================================

Titel:
Eliminating the Dark Sector: Unifying the Curvature Feedback Model
with MOND -- A Baryon-Only Universe with Geometric Dark Matter
and Dark Energy

Autoren:
Geiger, Lukas (Independent Researcher, Bernau im Schwarzwald, Germany)

Abstract:
We extend the Curvature Feedback Model (CFM) to a baryon-only universe
(Omega_m = Omega_b = 0.05) compatible with Modified Newtonian Dynamics
(MOND). The extended Friedmann equation H^2(a) = H_0^2[Omega_b a^{-3}
+ Phi_0 f_sat(a) + alpha a^{-beta} S(a)] replaces both dark energy
(saturation term) and dark matter (power-law term) with purely
geometric effects. A trace-coupling mechanism (S(a) -> 0 for radiation)
ensures consistency with Big Bang Nucleosynthesis. Tested against 1,590
Pantheon+ Type Ia supernovae, this dark-sector-free model achieves
chi^2 = 702.7 (Delta chi^2 = -26.3 vs. LambdaCDM, Delta AIC = -16.3,
Delta BIC = -4.2). MCMC posterior analysis reveals beta = 2.02 +/- 0.20,
indicating that the geometric dark matter term scales as spatial
curvature (a^{-2}, w = -1/3) -- not as matter. We introduce the
Decaying Dark Geometry hypothesis: dark matter and dark energy are two
phases of a single geometric relaxation process. The three principal
challenges (CMB acoustic peaks, Bullet Cluster, structure formation)
are addressed through the geometric phase transition framework.

Keywords:
cosmology; dark matter; dark energy; MOND; baryon-only universe;
Curvature Feedback Model; Pantheon+; geometric cosmology;
modified gravity; Decaying Dark Geometry; trace coupling

Titel:
Microscopic Foundations of the Curvature Feedback Model:
From Quantum Geometry to Macroscopic Saturation

Autoren:
Geiger, Lukas (Independent Researcher, Bernau im Schwarzwald, Germany)

Abstract:
We address the microscopic origin of the Curvature Feedback Model's
saturation ODE dOmega/da = k[1-(Omega/Phi_0)^2]. We derive the
effective Lagrangian L_CFM = R + gamma R^2 - (1/2)(d phi)^2 -
V_0/cosh^2(phi/phi_0) (Poeschl-Teller potential) and explore five
quantum gravity connections: Loop Quantum Gravity, Finsler geometry,
information-theoretic spacetime, causal set theory, and quantum error
correction. We propose a fractal game theory in which Nash equilibria
operate self-similarly across three levels (spacetime bits, particles,
cosmos). The geometric phase transition from dark-matter-like (a^{-2})
to dark-energy-like (tanh) behavior is interpreted as a Ginzburg-Landau
transition. Existing hints of cosmic birefringence (beta = 0.35 deg,
2.4 sigma, Planck data) are identified as a potential signature of
spin-network saturation. Testable predictions include gravitational
slip, scalar field oscillations, and modified gravitational wave
propagation.

Keywords:
quantum gravity; Curvature Feedback Model; Lagrangian formulation;
Loop Quantum Gravity; Finsler geometry; Poeschl-Teller potential;
cosmic birefringence; fractal game theory; phase transition;
quantum error correction
